## Naming / Register Pass

This pass only adjusts naming and register realism. It does **not** change plot, episode map, or structure.

| Previous register | MVP register |
|---|---|
| 女主正式中文名 | **Annie** as primary working name; Chinese legal name can stay off-page or appear only in formal contexts if needed later |
| 沈砚 | **Alan Shen / 沈总** |
| 陆沉 | **David Lu / David** |
| 梁太太 / 温太太 / 孟太太 / 许太太 | Keep surname + 太太 register |
| 都市言情式人物称呼 | Replace with overseas Chinese service-context names and workplace address forms |

Naming principle:

- Annie is a service professional, not an idol-drama heroine.
- 太太们 are mostly addressed by surname + 太太 because the story enters them through service, trust, and social distance.
- Male resource figures are addressed in realistic work/social registers: Alan Shen / 沈总, David Lu / David.
- Avoid overly polished romance names unless used deliberately as someone’s self-branding or social persona later.

# 0. MVP Objective

The 3-post MVP tests whether 《候鸟太太俱乐部》 can move from series bible into prose drafting without becoming either a customer gossip collection or a dry overseas service procedural.

| Post | Main test | What it must prove |
|---|---|---|
| Post 1 | 顾问视角能否建立高净值家庭秘密感 | Annie 的服务视角可以承载“体面家庭的不安全感”，并且女主不是旁观八卦，而是在做策略判断。 |
| Post 2 | 太太圈是否有平台传播力 | 太太之间的观察、试探、资源比较和女性网络有足够阅读快感。 |
| Post 3 | 女主主线是否成立 | 公司/老板/合同压力能把故事从单元 case 推向女主成长线，Annie 有边界和反向控制能力。 |

MVP hypothesis:

> If the first three posts can establish a repeatable triangle of **case conflict + female network + Annie’s boundary growth**, the series is ready for Post 1 prose draft.

---

# 1. MVP Success Criteria

The 3-post MVP is successful if it meets most of the following criteria:

| Criterion | Pass signal | Fail signal |
|---|---|---|
| Platform readability | Each post has a clear social-media-friendly situation: school crisis, afternoon tea, company pressure. | The outline reads like a consulting case memo. |
| 金丝雀文学情绪浓度 | Each post contains dependency, being needed, being observed, being priced, or hidden control. | It only describes rich-family logistics. |
| Female network potential | 太太们 are not interchangeable clients; each has desire, resource, and risk. | 太太们 only function as gossip sources. |
| Annie’s agency | Annie makes at least one clear strategy move per post. | Annie only listens, comforts, or reacts. |
| Controlled emotional tension | Each post has emotional pull: trust, pressure, admiration, dependence, or boundary danger. | Emotional layer is absent or becomes romance-first. |
| Mainline continuity | Post 1 secret → Post 2 network → Post 3 company extraction form a causal chain. | The posts can be read in any order with no loss. |
| Naming/register realism | Names and address forms feel like overseas Chinese service/work contexts. | Names feel like urban-romance character branding. |
| Safety boundary | Immigration, assets, privacy, school, family conflict remain narrative functions only. | The outline gives operational advice or procedural details. |
| Originality | Uses the playbook mechanisms without echoing source sample characters, relations, or events. | It feels like transplanted original-work dynamics. |

Minimum pass:

- Annie has a strategy action in all 3 posts.
- Each post ends with a new variable that changes the next post.
- Post 3 clearly introduces the central series conflict: Annie’s trust labor vs company monetization.
- No post depends on title formula or imitation of a specific author’s voice.

# 2. Post 1 Outline

## Working episode function

Post 1 establishes Annie’s professional POV and the first private trust breach: a high-net-worth family’s visible problem is school paperwork, but the real problem is that 温太太 does not control the household information she is expected to manage.

## Test target

**顾问视角能否建立高净值家庭秘密感。**

## Required elements

| Element | Outline design |
|---|---|
| 入学材料危机 | 温太太’s child has an admissions-related document issue shortly before a school deadline. The family looks fully prepared from the outside, but one missing page exposes internal chaos. No procedural school or immigration advice is given. |
| 温太太私密请求 | 温太太 asks Annie not to escalate the emotional part of the crisis to her husband or to the company’s general client notes. The request is not about hiding a crime; it is about preserving dignity and avoiding household blame. |
| Annie 被需要的危险 | Annie realizes that being the only person 温太太 dares to call feels validating, but it also pulls her into a private family power structure that is not written into her job description. |
| 具体物质/资源细节 | A leather document pouch with color-coded tabs, a child’s school blazer still in plastic, a scanned lease file with the husband’s assistant copied, and an unused lunch-account card. Pick 1–2 in drafting, not all. |
| Annie 的策略动作 | Annie separates the situation into three layers: school-facing missing item, company-facing task record, and private emotional context. She records only necessary service facts, not 温太太’s fear or family blame language. |
| controlled emotional tension | 温太太’s line of trust is emotionally intense: she treats Annie less like a vendor and more like the only safe witness. Annie wants to help, but notices that being needed can become a trap. |
| ending new variable | 温太太 says this cannot go into the company notes because “he checks those too.” This implies the husband’s remote control extends into service documentation. |
| in-series hook line | “Annie 那天第一次发现，有些家庭的秘密不在保险柜里，而在孩子入学文件少掉的那一页。” |

## Episode beats

| Beat | Function |
|---|---|
| 1. Normal service entry | Annie receives what looks like a routine school-material emergency. Establish her job without over-explaining the industry. |
| 2. Visible wealth, invisible fragility | The family has every paid service, but 温太太 cannot confidently answer one practical question. |
| 3. 温太太 lowers her voice | She asks Annie to help before her husband finds out. This turns service into trust. |
| 4. Annie creates boundaries | She handles the service task but refuses to turn private panic into a sales/client note. |
| 5. New variable | The husband’s visibility into company notes makes Annie realize privacy itself is part of the power structure. |

## Potential risks and fixes

| Risk | Fix |
|---|---|
| Too procedural / school-admin heavy | Keep the document issue as surface conflict only; spend more attention on 温太太’s lack of control and Annie’s documentation choice. |
| Too much client gossip | Do not reveal unnecessary family details. Focus on what Annie sees, what she chooses not to record, and why. |
| Annie too passive | Make her strategy action explicit: triage, minimal record, boundary between service facts and private fear. |
| Operational risk | Do not specify exact admissions process, document names beyond generic narrative objects, or how to bypass any system. |
| Emotion too light | Let the danger come from being trusted: Annie feels useful, then immediately understands the cost. |

# 3. Post 2 Outline

## Working episode function

Post 2 moves Annie from one-family service into the edge of the 太太 network. The afternoon tea is not social reward; it is an interview, a pricing session, and a silent test of whether Annie can be used without becoming dangerous.

## Test target

**太太圈是否有平台传播力。**

## Required elements

| Element | Outline design |
|---|---|
| 下午茶场景 | 梁太太 invites Annie to a quiet weekday afternoon tea at a private-feeling hotel lounge / membership restaurant in a fictional coastal district. The atmosphere is soft; the questions are not. |
| 梁太太带 Annie 进入圈层 | 梁太太 frames it as thanks for helping 温太太, but actually introduces Annie to a small circle of 太太们 who want to know whether she is reliable, discreet, and useful. |
| 太太之间的试探 | 梁太太 is warm but possessive; 孟太太 is direct and tests Annie’s principles; 许太太 smiles and asks questions that are too specific. 温太太 appears more careful now that others are present. |
| Annie 被观察/被定价 | Annie notices she is being evaluated through her bag, car situation, accent, phone model, and what she refuses to say. She is not only serving the table; she is being priced by it. |
| 具体物质/资源细节 | Three-tier tea stand, valet tags, private-school pickup calendar on someone’s phone, a spare chair occupied by a designer bag, and a quiet comparison of which district each family lives in. Pick selectively. |
| Annie 的策略动作 | Annie does not sell services or trade secrets. She answers in categories rather than names: “school rhythm,” “housing readiness,” “emergency contact,” “authorized communication.” She gathers patterns without collecting gossip. |
| controlled emotional tension | 梁太太 wants Annie to be “her person”; 孟太太 wants to see if Annie can say no; 许太太 wants to know what Annie knows. Annie feels the pull of belonging but recognizes the cost of being owned by the circle. |
| ending new variable | Annie realizes each 太太 has told her, the company, and their husband a different version of the same life. The circle is not just social; it is an alternate information system. |
| in-series hook line | “那张下午茶桌上，最便宜的是点心，最贵的是每个人没说完的后半句。” |

## Episode beats

| Beat | Function |
|---|---|
| 1. Invitation as reward | 梁太太 invites Annie in a tone that sounds friendly but carries selection power. |
| 2. Social surface | Beautiful table, soft lighting, polite compliments. Establish platform-friendly visual scene. |
| 3. Test begins | Questions drift from school and housing into “what do your clients usually do when…” Annie sees the trap. |
| 4. Annie refuses gossip format | She gives resource categories and general service principles, not client-specific secrets. |
| 5. Female network emerges | 太太们 understand that Annie is useful precisely because she does not leak easily. |
| 6. New variable | Annie sees the three-version structure: husband version, company version, women’s version. |

## Potential risks and fixes

| Risk | Fix |
|---|---|
| 太太圈 becomes flat rich-women gossip | Give each 太太 a distinct pressure: 梁太太 wants exclusivity, 孟太太 wants rules, 许太太 wants leverage, 温太太 wants safety. |
| Too urban-romance in tone | Use surnames and service-context observations; avoid ornate personal introductions. |
| Annie only observes | Let her make a tactical refusal: she redirects name-based questions into resource-based answers. |
| Emotional tension too weak | Emphasize being invited vs being captured: Annie wants access, but access has ownership pressure. |
| Platform scene becomes luxury display | Every object should reveal status, access, or anxiety, not just wealth. |

# 4. Post 3 Outline

## Working episode function

Post 3 turns the series from service cases into Annie’s mainline conflict. The company recognizes her value but tries to convert her private trust with 太太们 into CRM data and sales opportunities. Alan Shen / 沈总 becomes a sustained resource pressure source: he praises her, promotes her, and tries to own what makes her valuable.

## Test target

**女主主线是否成立。**

## Required elements

| Element | Outline design |
|---|---|
| 公司要求把客户隐私转化为销售线索 | After Post 1 and Post 2, Alan Shen / 沈总 asks Annie to turn “soft signals” from 太太们 into upgrade opportunities, renewal risk categories, and family-decision-maker mapping. Keep it narrative; no actual CRM or sales process instructions. |
| Alan Shen / 沈总的认可与控制 | Alan praises Annie as unusually good with high-end families, then asks for more detailed notes. Recognition and extraction arrive in the same conversation. |
| Annie 第一次明确设边界 | Annie says she can summarize service needs, risk categories, and authorized requests, but she will not upload private emotional disclosures or unverified family conflict into a shared sales file. |
| 具体物质/资源细节 | Glass conference room, CRM dashboard, client tier labels, renewal package deck, bonus calculation sheet, Annie’s company laptop reflected in the glass wall. Pick 1–2. |
| controlled emotional tension | Alan’s approval matters because Annie wants career safety and recognition. The danger is not romantic surrender; it is professional intimacy becoming control. |
| ending new variable | Alan tells her: “Then write me a version you are willing to stand behind.” This gives Annie room to define her own framework, but also puts her under formal scrutiny. |
| in-series hook line | “沈总给她的不是一道命令，是一个更危险的机会：把她知道的秘密，改写成她自己的规则。” |

## Episode beats

| Beat | Function |
|---|---|
| 1. Post-afternoon-tea fallout | Annie returns to work with a clearer sense that 太太们 trust her more than the company does. |
| 2. Alan recognizes the value | Alan notices client retention or engagement improving around Annie’s accounts. He calls her in. |
| 3. Praise becomes extraction | He says she has instinct, then asks her to make the instinct legible to the company. |
| 4. Annie sees the conversion | What 太太们 gave her as trust is being reframed as sales intelligence. |
| 5. First boundary statement | Annie offers a structured but privacy-preserving service summary. She refuses client-specific emotional disclosures. |
| 6. New variable | Alan does not fire her; he asks her to formalize the boundary. This creates the next arc: can she turn boundaries into a product/service method? |

## Potential risks and fixes

| Risk | Fix |
|---|---|
| Alan becomes generic霸总 | Keep him professional, practical, and partly right. His danger is managerial ownership, not romantic dominance. |
| Company conflict feels too corporate | Tie every corporate ask back to Post 1 and Post 2: 温太太’s request, 太太圈’s trust, Annie’s notes. |
| Annie’s boundary too preachy | Let her make a concrete distinction: service needs yes; private fear no. |
| Operational risk around privacy/sales | Keep all systems generic. Do not describe real data handling, compliance steps, or actual privacy workarounds. |
| Emotion too diluted | Annie should feel tempted by recognition: Alan sees her value before anyone else at work does. That is why the boundary costs something. |

# 5. Continuity Across 3 Posts

The three posts should form one continuous escalation rather than three isolated cases:

```text
Post 1: One 太太 privately trusts Annie.
        ↓
Post 2: The 太太 circle tests whether Annie can become a shared trust node.
        ↓
Post 3: The company notices Annie’s trust value and tries to convert it into sales/control.
```

## Continuity chain

| Thread | Post 1 | Post 2 | Post 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annie’s role | Service executor | Observed trust node | Boundary-setting professional |
| Main pressure | 温太太’s private request | 太太圈’s social pricing | Company’s data/sales extraction |
| Emotional tension | Being needed feels dangerous | Being invited feels like being claimed | Being recognized feels like being owned |
| Power gap | Annie vs paying family structure | Annie vs 太太 network hierarchy | Annie vs company / Alan Shen |
| Material detail | School document pouch | Afternoon tea / social objects | CRM / renewal deck / glass meeting room |
| New variable | Husband may see company notes | 太太们 operate with three versions of truth | Annie must define what can and cannot be converted into company knowledge |

## 追更动力

By the end of Post 3, readers should want to know:

- Will Annie’s privacy-preserving framework protect her or make her a target?
- Will 太太们 trust her more because she refused to expose them?
- Will Alan Shen reward her boundary or try to absorb it into the company?
- Can Annie turn invisible trust labor into a visible professional method?

# 6. Risk Review

## 6.1 是否太像客户八卦合集

| Assessment | Notes | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Medium risk | Post 1 and Post 2 both involve client secrets. | Anchor every secret to Annie’s decision: what she records, refuses, redirects, or protects. |

The MVP should not ask “what is this family’s secret?” It should ask:

> What does Annie do once someone gives her a secret she should not fully own?

## 6.2 是否情绪浓度不足

| Assessment | Notes | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Medium risk | The concept is service/network-heavy, so romance and恨海情天浓度 can dilute. | Use controlled emotional tension: trust, dependence, professional praise, social invitation, and the danger of being needed. |

The first 3 posts do not need heavy romance. They need emotional hooks:

- 温太太: “only you can know this.”
- 梁太太: “come sit with us.”
- Alan Shen: “you are good at this; now give it to the company.”

## 6.3 是否名字/语气太都市言情

| Assessment | Notes | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Low after naming pass | Annie, 温太太, 梁太太, Alan Shen / 沈总, David Lu / David fit the service context better. | Avoid introducing full lyrical Chinese names unless needed for formal documents. Use address forms and workplace names. |

## 6.4 是否有操作性风险

| Assessment | Notes | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Manageable | School, privacy, family documentation, and client data are sensitive areas. | Keep them as narrative pressure sources only. Do not provide process steps, legal/immigration advice, asset handling, monitoring methods, or real institutional names. |

## 6.5 是否女主过于工具人

| Assessment | Notes | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Medium risk | Annie is a service provider, so she can become only a helper. | Each post must include her strategy action and internal growth: triage, refusal, translation, boundary. |

Anti-tool-person rule:

> Annie must change the situation, not merely witness it.

## 6.6 是否 romance dilution 仍然存在

| Assessment | Notes | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Present but acceptable for MVP | The first 3 posts prioritize network formation and company pressure over direct romance. | Treat Alan’s approval and 梁太太’s possessiveness as emotional entanglement. David can be seeded later, not forced into Post 1–3 unless needed. |

Romance-dilution prevention for MVP:

- Post 1: emotional intimacy through 温太太’s private request.
- Post 2: social possession through 梁太太 bringing Annie into the circle.
- Post 3: career intimacy and control through Alan Shen’s recognition.

This is enough for MVP. Do not force a conventional love interest too early.

# 7. Draft Readiness Decision

## Decision: Ready to draft Post 1

Reason:

- The 3-post arc has a clear escalation: private trust → female network → company extraction.
- Annie has concrete strategy actions in all three posts.
- The MVP preserves the series bible’s strengths: female network, service-context realism, power gap, material detail, and boundary growth.
- The naming/register pass reduces urban-romance risk.
- Controlled emotional tension exists without making Annie恋爱脑.
- Safety boundaries are clear: sensitive areas function as narrative pressure, not operational instruction.

## Drafting recommendation for Post 1

Post 1 should be drafted as a **platform-native narrative outline expansion**, not a full polished literary chapter at first.

Draft priorities:

1. Establish Annie’s working eye in the first 3–5 paragraphs.
2. Make the入学材料危机 concrete but not procedural.
3. Let 温太太’s private request create the emotional hook.
4. Make Annie’s strategy action visible.
5. End on the company-note / husband-visibility new variable.
6. Avoid overexplaining the whole series world.

Post 1 minimum viable ending:

> The reader should finish Post 1 thinking: “This is not about school paperwork. This is about who gets to know the truth inside a rich family.”
